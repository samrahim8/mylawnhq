## SECTION 9: VISUAL DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Reference: `/mnt/.skills/skills/visual-taste/SKILL.md`

### The World We're In

**Pick a world and commit:** LawnHQ should feel like a **sun-drenched backyard in late May**â€”warm, unhurried, the golden hour before a weekend cookout. Not sterile tech. Not generic SaaS. Not "clean and modern."

### Palette (Warm, Grounded)

| Element | Color | Hex | Rationale |
|---------|-------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | Lawn Green | `#4A6741` | Mature grass, not neon. Earned, not applied. |
| Accent | Terracotta | `#C4785A` | Warm soil, clay pots, aged patios |
| Background | Cream | `#F5F2EB` | Sun-bleached, not stark white |
| Text | Deep Brown | `#2D2A26` | Feels like garden journal ink |
| Secondary | Ochre | `#D4A857` | Golden hour warmth |

**Avoid:** Gray-beige hedging, sterile whites, tech-blue, neon greens

### One Bold Move

The **terracotta CTA buttons** are the checkered tile border. That's the signature. Everything else stays quietâ€”cream backgrounds, muted greens, simple typography.

Don't add:
- Gradient overlays
- Multiple accent colors
- Decorative illustrations everywhere
- Italics for "elegance"

### Texture as Signature

Reference **real-world lawn care textures**:
- Aged brass (watering cans, hose fittings)
- Terracotta (pots, borders)
- Weathered wood (fence posts, garden stakes)
- Canvas/linen (gardening gloves, bags)

Use these in:
- Card backgrounds (subtle linen texture)
- Borders (terracotta-toned lines)
- Icons (brass/copper tones, not flat gray)

### Typography

**Headline:** Serif or strong sans-serif with personality. Not geometric. Something that could be on a seed packet from the 1950s.

**Body:** Clean, readable, but warm. Avoid the default tech fonts (Inter, SF Pro). Consider:
- Freight Text
- Tiempos
- GT Sectra
- Or a friendly sans like SÃ¶hne

### Photography Style

- **Golden hour lighting** â€” warm, not harsh midday
- **Real lawns** â€” imperfect is fine, authentic is mandatory
- **Hands in frame** â€” people doing things, not posed
- **No stock photo energy** â€” nothing that reads as "happy homeowner" clip art

### What NOT to Do

| âŒ Avoid | âœ… Instead |
|---------|-----------|
| Emoji as decoration | Subtle icons or none |
| Gradients for "depth" | Flat, confident colors |
| Clip art illustrations | Photography or custom illustration |
| Tech-startup aesthetic | Garden shed aesthetic |
| "Clean and modern" | "Warm and particular" |
| Gray-beige everything | Commit to the terracotta/cream/green palette |

### The Test

Before finalizing any design, ask:

> "Does this feel like it belongs in the backyard of someone who actually cares about their lawnâ€”or does it feel like a SaaS dashboard?"

If the answer is dashboard, add more warmth. Remove the safe choices.
